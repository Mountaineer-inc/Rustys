Section 01

The Market, Ground-Truthed

US Anglers 2024
57.9M

All-time high since tracking began. 19% of the U.S. population. (RBFF 2025)

Freshwater Anglers
43M+

Roughly 2× the combined total of saltwater and fly fishing.

US Fishing Goods Market
~$9B

Baits & lures fastest-growing category at 6.7% CAGR. (Grand View Research, 2024)

US Prepared Catfish Bait
$75–150M

Directional estimate. No public pure-play number exists. Inferred from retail shelf share.

Channel, blue, and flathead are the big three catfish species. Channel cats are the most widely distributed freshwater fish in North America. Farm-raised channel catfish alone is a $400M+ aquaculture industry. Catfish has deep cultural and economic roots across the Mississippi/Missouri/Ohio river basins, TX/OK/KS/AR/MO/TN/KY/AL/MS/GA/SC/NC/VA/MD, and the Chesapeake watershed. This is American catfish country — not a regional story.

Reality Check

We're in a niche within a niche. $9B fishing isn't our TAM. The realistic view: 8–12M U.S. anglers target catfish with some regularity. 2–4M are serious. Of those, maybe 1–2M spend $30–150/year on branded prepared bait. Rusty's Year 3 target of $325–400K is 0.3–0.5% of the estimated U.S. prepared catfish bait category. Achievable, not heroic.

"THE catfish bait company" is a 5–10 year positioning play, not a Year 3 claim. We earn the market share over time.

Demographic Grounding

From RBFF 2025, NOAA data, and state creel surveys: 63% male / 37% female overall fishing, with catfish skewing more male (but female share meaningful at ~25–30%). Peak participation ages 35–54, strong second bracket 55–74. Hispanic participation doubled in the last decade (3.3M → 6.6M). Black angler participation at all-time high (5.5M). Catfish specifically has strong Black and Hispanic participation in Southeast and urban waterways. Dedicated prepared-bait buyers skew $40–90K HHI.

The Churn Reality

Fishing lost 16.6M anglers in 2024 — a –23% churn rate. Avid participation has slowly declined (37% fished monthly+ a decade ago, 32% today). This means retention is worth more than acquisition. Family and youth content has outsized long-term value — 85% of current anglers fished before age 12. Subscriptions, variety packs, and email flows are the hedge against industry churn.

Where They Live in Media

Different from bass anglers. Different from fly fishermen. The catfish community has its own center of gravity. Serious catfish anglers live on YouTube — content library is deep, community is engaged, algorithm rewards niche. Facebook is where they talk to each other. Forums move authority, not volume.

Section 05

Brand Operating Philosophy

Two frameworks, working together.

StoryBrand (Donald Miller) gives us structure. Customer is the hero. Rusty's is the guide. Every piece of communication answers: who's the hero, what's the problem, how do we help, what's the plan, what happens if they act, what happens if they don't. Clarity over cleverness. If the customer can't locate themselves in the story, we've already lost.

Bernays gives us depth. People don't buy products. They buy what products let them become. A jar of Rusty's in the boat isn't bait — it's a signal to the other guys at the ramp about what kind of angler you are. Our job is to engineer the context so the right voices say the right things in the right settings, and the symbolic meaning travels.

The Framework Logic

Miller without Bernays reads flat. Bernays without Miller drifts into manipulation. Together: structure plus meaning. The BrandScript gives us the skeleton. The Bernays layer gives it weight.

Section 06

The BrandScript

Figure 05
StoryBrand Framework — Applied to Rusty's
Miller StoryBrand framework applied to Rusty's brand narrative. The plan (Step 4) becomes the structure for the website "How It Works" section, packaging, and content strategy.

The Character — Hero

The catfish angler. Weekend enthusiast to tournament serious. Across American catfish country. 35–65 primarily. What they want: more fish in the cooler, more confidence in what they're putting on the hook. A clean answer to the question every catfish angler asks at 4 AM on the bank: "Am I using the right bait?"

The Guide

Rusty's is not the hero. Rusty's is the guide who makes the hero successful. Empathy: We know what it's like to drive two hours to the water and wonder if your bait is the reason. We fish too. Authority: Rusty's formulations have been producing fish for decades. The recipes are proven. The product is made the same way every time.

The Call to Action

Direct: Shop the core lineup. Subscribe and save. Order the variety pack. Transitional: Download the free Rusty's Guide to Catfish Bait — a short PDF that helps anglers choose the right bait for their water, regardless of brand. Email in, value out, trust built.

Section 07

Positioning

Rusty's makes prepared catfish bait for anglers who'd rather be fishing than experimenting. Proven formulations, made the same way every time, built for the guys who actually show up to the water.
Internal Strategy Compass

For catfish anglers who've been let down by inconsistent bait and corporate brands that don't fish, Rusty's is the guide that delivers proven formulations backed by real anglers and real results — unlike Triple S (cheap shelf filler) or Team Catfish (premium but remote), Rusty's builds authority through the community it serves, not by talking down to it.

Section 08

Brand Pillars

Four non-negotiables. Every decision runs through these.

Pillar 01
Proven, not promised.

Every formulation earned its place through fish in the cooler. We show the work. We publish catches. We don't overclaim.

Pillar 02
Angler-first, not angler-facing.

Brands that fish vs. brands that sell to fishermen. Everything is built by, tested by, and shaped by actual catfish anglers.

Pillar 03
Made to work, not to move units.

We don't reformulate to hit a price point. We don't water things down when costs go up. Consistency is the product.

Pillar 04
The community is the brand.

Customer catches are the primary content. Pro-staff are the face. Forums and groups are where the brand actually lives.

Section 09

The Bernays Layer — Symbolic Meaning

What does Rusty's stand for beyond the product? What are customers buying when they buy it?

They're buying membership in a specific kind of angler identity. Not the guy at Walmart grabbing whatever's on sale. Not the corporate tournament pro with logos head-to-toe. The guy who's been at this long enough to know what works. The guy whose buddies ask what he's using. The guy who shows up early, stays late, and has a jar of Rusty's in the cooler because he's done experimenting.

The jar in the boat is the signal. Yeti didn't sell coolers. They sold the identity of the guy who buys a real cooler. Duck Camp doesn't sell shirts. They sell the identity of a certain kind of outdoorsman. Rusty's sells the identity of the catfish angler who's figured it out.

Who Needs to Hold the Jar

Pro-staff on their YouTube videos. Tournament winners in their weigh-in photos. Respected voices in catfish forums mentioning it in replies. Guides using it with their clients. Regular customers posting their catches with the jar in frame. This is the engineered context — not advertising. Positioning the product where the audience already trusts the messenger.

What Rusty's Never Is

Celebrity-endorsed by people who don't fish · Shouty or hype-driven · Positioned around scarcity games or hypebeast mechanics · Self-referential (we don't talk about ourselves — we talk about anglers)

Section 10

Voice & Tone

How It Sounds
  • Competent professional having a conversation. Not a pitchman.
  • Confident without being arrogant. Knowledgeable without condescending.
  • Direct. No filler. No corporate hedge words.
  • Warm when warranted. Serious when the topic calls for it.
  • Uses contractions. Varies sentence length.
  • Writes how an actual catfish angler actually talks.

More like a guy at the tackle shop who actually knows the answer than either a pitchman or a best friend.

How It Never Sounds
  • Corporate ("leveraging our proprietary formulation technology")
  • Marketing-speak ("unlock the power of")
  • Condescending ("did you know...")
  • Performatively folksy ("howdy there, partner")
  • Hype-driven (exclamation points, all caps, emoji abuse)
  • Self-congratulatory

Gut check: would a guy who's been catfishing for 30 years roll his eyes? If yes, rewrite it.

Section 11

Visual Identity Direction

Design decisions get made in Month 3–4 with the designer. This sets the brief. Feel: Heritage but not dated. Rugged but not cartoonish. Professional but not corporate. Catfish-specific, not generic outdoor. Colors: Earth tones and water tones. Browns, tans, muted greens, deep blues, teals. No neon. A defensible, recognizable color story that reads on a shelf and on a phone. Photography: Real anglers. Real catches. Real water. Morning light, evening light, the actual weathered hands of guys who fish. No stock.

Section 12

Packaging Principles

Packaging is media. It sits on the bank, in the boat, in the photos.

Wholesale Packaging

Shelf-stable, professional, catches the eye from three feet away. Clear species callout (channel, blue, flathead). Net weight, product name, key benefit front and center. Usage instructions and ingredients on back. Made in USA. Designed to be read in a bait shop, not a boutique.

DTC Packaging

Same core product packaging, shipped in a branded outer box. Unboxing: branded tissue, sticker pack, simple thank-you card written in Rusty's voice. Not a luxury unboxing — a thoughtful one. Would a catfish angler actually use what comes in the box? If yes, include it. If no, cut it.

What Packaging Must Do

1. Look right in a photo (Instagram, tournament weigh-in, customer posts) · 2. Look right on a shelf next to Triple S and Team Catfish · 3. Survive a wet boat, a hot truck bed, a muddy bank · 4. Explain itself to a new user without making veterans feel talked down to

Section 13

Our Story

Short narrative for the website, press kit, pro-staff onboarding, and retail conversations. Customer-as-hero frame throughout.

Catfish anglers have been getting taken advantage of for a long time. Brands that don't fish. Formulations that change when the accountants get involved. Premium prices for bait that works when it feels like it. We're tired of it. So we're building something different. Rusty's formulations have been producing fish for decades — the same way, every time, because the recipe is the recipe. We're not the cheapest option. We're the one that works.
Copywriting Note

This deliberately avoids founding-date claims or "40-year heritage" language we can't yet substantiate. Works on what's actually true: the formulations are proven, they're consistent, and the brand is being built in service of anglers. When DD confirms actual founding history, we can sharpen the timeline.

Section 15

What the Brand Will Not Do

Saying no is the strategy.

The No List
  • Chase trends — gimmicky SKUs, viral stunts, hype drops
  • Discount our way to volume — kills margin and brand equity
  • Sponsor personalities who don't actually fish catfish
  • Make claims we can't back up with fish
  • Water down formulations to hit a price point
  • Expand into adjacent species before we've earned catfish authority
  • Chase Bass Pro reactivation before DTC proves demand
  • Copy Team Catfish, Triple S, or anyone else — we build our own lane
