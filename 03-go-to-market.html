How Rusty's reaches $125–150K Year 1 with DTC at 60% of mix

Prepared for Joseph Skinner

Framework Pro-Staff as growth engine
Section 01

The Operating Thesis

Five things, in this order, drive everything Rusty's does in Year 1.

Pro-Staff is the growth engine. Creator-led authority is the fastest and cheapest way for a niche brand to earn trust in the catfish space.
01
Pro-Staff is the growth engine

Creator-led authority is the fastest and cheapest way for a niche brand to earn trust in the catfish space. Everything else supports it.

02
Community builds the moat

Facebook groups, forums, tournament scenes — this is where catfish anglers actually live. Show up authentically, serve the community, and the product sells itself over time.

03
Content is the capital

Every video, photo, post, and blog the Pro-Staff and customers create is an asset that keeps working. Invest in helping them create good stuff, repurpose relentlessly.

04
Paid amplifies what already works

We don't run paid ads to cold audiences until we have proof — creator content that lands, customer reviews, organic traction. Then we pour gasoline on what's burning.

05
Retail grows from demand

Existing wholesale relationships get rebuilt. New retail expands when DTC pull-through creates inbound interest. Bass Pro and similar return on our timeline.

Section 02

The Pro-Staff Program

Centerpiece of the marketing plan. Everything downstream connects to it.

Figure 01
Pro-Staff Three-Tier Pyramid

Three tiers create aspiration. Movement between tiers generates social signaling and content momentum — the Bernays mechanic applied to a creator program.

Three-Tier Structure

Pro-Staff (Top Tier)

  • 2–3 creators on annual contracts
  • Authority voices in the catfish space (Catfish Edge-level credibility)
  • National geographic spread — one Mississippi basin, one Southeast, one Midwest or Chesapeake
  • Comp: Annual retainer + performance bonuses + affiliate revenue share
  • Commitment: Minimum content cadence, tournament presence where applicable, first look at new SKUs, direct line to the brand

Field Staff (Working Middle)

  • 10–15 creators and guides across catfish country
  • Regional creators with engaged audiences (2K–25K subs/followers)
  • Product seeding + per-piece compensation + affiliate
  • Commitment: Regular content featuring Rusty's, tagged posts, participation in seasonal campaigns

Rusty's Ambassadors (Community Layer)

  • Open-tier, application-based after Month 6
  • Tournament winners, respected forum voices, loyal customers with a platform
  • Free product + custom discount code + social recognition + early SKU access
  • Commitment: Use the product, tag Rusty's, represent the brand in community spaces

Why Three Tiers

Creates aspiration. A Field Staff member can earn their way to Pro-Staff. An Ambassador can earn their way to Field Staff. This is the Bernays play — the tiers themselves create social signaling, and movement between them generates content and momentum.

Recruitment Timeline

  • Month 2–3 — Pro-Staff Manager hired or contracted (Joseph's network contact). First conversations with authority creators.
  • Month 4–5 — Field Staff soft launch. 10–15 creators contacted privately, sent product, invited to try before launch. No public roster yet.
  • Month 6 — DTC launch. Pro-Staff publicly announced. Field Staff begins posting. Launch content hits with 10–15 creators simultaneously — borrowed audiences on day one.
  • Month 7–9 — Ambassador program opens publicly. Application process with clear standards. First cohort announced Month 9–10.
  • Month 12 — First Pro-Staff review. Performance assessment, contract renewals, roster adjustments.

Standards — Written, Not Implied

Every tier has a written agreement. The Pro-Staff agreement covers:

  • Minimum content cadence (2 Rusty's-featuring pieces per month for Pro-Staff, 1 for Field Staff)
  • Content quality standards (not a shot list — use the product, be authentic, tag correctly)
  • Attendance at one Rusty's-supported tournament per year (Pro-Staff only)
  • Social tagging and response expectations
  • Exclusivity terms (no direct competitor sponsorships — Triple S, Team Catfish, Magic)
  • Termination terms (unresponsiveness, dead accounts, brand misrepresentation)
Infrastructure Note

The standards document is key infrastructure. Without it, the program drifts into "free product for whoever asks." Treating agreements as optional is how creator programs die.

Section 03

Channel Strategy

Figure 02
Channel Strategy Matrix

Rust entries indicate delayed activation — channel is known but deliberately held until content library and proof metrics exist.

Channel Detail

Primary Content Engine

YouTube

Role: Highest-intent audience. Search visibility lasts for years.

  • Pro-Staff and Field Staff content is the primary output — 100+ pieces featuring Rusty's across the roster in Year 1
  • Rusty's owned YouTube is a community highlight reel, not an owned authority channel — amplifying the pro-staff, not competing with them
  • Repurpose pro-staff content on Rusty's owned channels with permission
  • Monthly "Rusty's Roundup" — short clip reel of the best pro-staff catches of the month

Key metric: Pro-staff content pieces published featuring Rusty's. Views are secondary.

Community Layer

Facebook

Role: Community, not sales.

  • Business page with consistent posting (3–4x/week) — catches, tips, pro-staff spotlights, product education
  • Joseph or pro-staff manager joins 20–30 catfishing groups as an individual, not as brand
  • Contribute value for 60 days before any brand mention
  • Regional group engagement — comment on catches, answer questions, be a knowledgeable presence
  • Brand page runs targeted ads only after Month 7 (once content library exists)

Key metric: Unprompted Rusty's mentions in groups we don't own. That's the signal the community is adopting the brand.

Visual Storytelling

Instagram

  • 3–4x/week posts, 1–2 Reels/week
  • Heavy UGC and pro-staff content (with permission and tags)
  • Tournament coverage, weigh-ins, regional catches
  • Stories for behind-the-scenes, day-to-day, pro-staff spotlights
  • Hashtags: #catfishing #catfish #catfishbait #stinkbait #channelcat #bluecat #flatheadcat

Key metric: Engagement rate and shares. Not follower count.

Authority & Credibility

Forums

  • Joseph or pro-staff manager establishes presence on Catfish1/USCA, 2Cool Catfish Lounge, Lake-Link, Reddit r/catfishing
  • Posts under personal name with brand disclosed in profile
  • Contributes expertise, answers questions, shares catches
  • Never posts promotional content — the forum culture will punish it instantly
  • Brand visibility comes from signature lines, profile info, earned mentions from others

Key metric: Unsolicited Rusty's mentions by other forum members.

Legitimacy & Regional Presence

Tournaments

Tournament Approach

Year 1: 8–12 small regional tournaments in catfish country (NC, SC, VA, TN, KY, MO, KS, TX). $100–500 sponsor contributions per event. Bait for prize bags, product for judges. Pro-staff and Field Staff attend where geographically aligned. Content captured at every event.

Year 2: Regional circuits ($1–3K sponsorship range). Consider hosting a Rusty's-branded tournament.

Year 3+: Evaluate national circuit (King Kat tier).

Key metric: Content generated per dollar spent. A $300 sponsorship that produces 10 pieces of pro-staff and ambassador content is a win even without direct attribution to sales.

Retention Layer

Email / Klaviyo

Where LTV lives.

  • Capture: Homepage pop-up (10% off first order), exit intent, blog/content upgrades, checkout
  • Flows: Welcome series (5 emails, brand story + product education + first-purchase incentive), Abandoned Cart (3 emails), Post-Purchase, Win-Back (60+ days inactive), Seasonal
  • Campaigns: 1–2x/week. New SKUs, tournament content, pro-staff spotlights, seasonal tips, limited releases
  • Segmentation: By product purchased, geography, engagement, purchase frequency

Key metric: Email-attributed revenue and repeat purchase rate.

Amplification — Month 7+

Paid Media (Facebook, Google)

Amplification layer, not acquisition layer. Active Month 7+ only.

  • Facebook/Instagram Ads — $10–12K Year 1 starting Month 7: Targeting catfishing interests, fishing content engagers, lookalikes of email list and pro-staff audiences. Creative: pro-staff content and customer testimonials — not corporate brand ads. Funnel: Cold → Video views → Retargeting → Conversion.
  • Google Ads — $5–7K Year 1 starting Month 7: Shopping (product listing ads), Search (branded + generic catfish bait terms), Retargeting (cart abandonment, site visitor follow-up).

Total paid Year 1: $15–19K — smaller than originally planned because the pro-staff program absorbs most of the envelope.

Key metric: ROAS (3:1 minimum target). Paid only scales when it's profitable.

Section 04

Content Strategy

What We Publish from Rusty's Owned Channels

Educational

  • How to fish different waters for different catfish
  • Seasonal patterns (spring pre-spawn, summer night, fall feed, winter deep)
  • Rig tutorials (slip bobber, carolina rig, float-paternoster, tight-line)
  • Species-specific (channel vs blue vs flathead behavior)
  • Bait selection guides (not just "use Rusty's" — actually helpful)

Community

  • Customer catch features (submitted via social tag or email)
  • Pro-staff spotlights
  • Tournament coverage
  • Behind-the-scenes production and team content (sparingly — we're not the hero)

Product

  • New SKU launches
  • Usage tips
  • Pairing with rigs and conditions
  • Pro Series development content (Month 9+)

Cadence

  • Blog — 2x/week Months 6–9 (build library), 1x/week Month 10+
  • Instagram — 3–4x/week + 1–2 Reels
  • Facebook — 3–4x/week (business page) + daily group engagement (personal account)
  • Email — 1–2x/week
  • YouTube — Monthly community roundup + repurposed pro-staff content as approved

Production

Who Creates

  • Pro-Staff and Field Staff — primary content source
  • Joseph — brand-owned content. Website copy, product descriptions, blog posts, email flows, ad copy, most photography and video. Creative production background handles in-house Year 1.
  • Easton — authentic fishing content as byproduct of his actual fishing. His catches with Rusty's are pro-staff-grade material at zero creator cost.
  • Customers — ongoing UGC from tagged posts and email submissions

Contract Support (Minimal)

  • Pro photographer for launch hero shoot, Month 4–5 — $1–2K, one-time professional shoot for lasting brand assets
  • Videographer for launch campaign, Month 5–6 — $1–2K, one or two key launch videos
Year 1 Content Contract Spend

$2–4K — dramatically reduced from original estimate because Joseph handles most of it. The trade-off: Joseph owning creative Year 1 keeps brand voice tight, saves $15–25K in contract costs, and means the brand evolves based on real use instead of agency deliverables. Downside: eats 5–10 hours of weekly time during launch prep (Months 3–5).

What We Don't Produce

  • Stock photography or generic fishing imagery
  • Over-produced corporate brand videos
  • Personality-driven "the Joseph show" content — customer is the hero
Section 05

Wholesale and Retail Marketing

Current Accounts

  • Month 2–3 — Direct outreach from Joseph. Confirm relationships, get product feedback, understand inventory and reorder patterns. Rebuild from mom-and-pop baseline.
  • Month 4–6 — Ongoing relationship management. POS materials developed — shelf talkers with QR codes to pro-staff videos, comparison cards, usage guides.
  • Month 6–12 — Co-op marketing where warranted. If Scheels or Big Rock wants a feature promotion or end-cap display, support with materials and modest co-op contribution.

Bass Pro Posture

Courtesy call post-acquisition. "New ownership transitioning operations. We'll be in touch as we relaunch. What are you looking for in the category?" Keeps the door open, sets tone, costs nothing.

Then back off. No chasing. Return only when:

  • DTC generating $8–10K+/month consistently
  • 100+ reviews (4+ stars)
  • 1,000+ engaged social followers
  • Professional packaging and brand in place
  • QC systems documented
  • Inventory capacity for 100–200 case initial order
  • Clean financials
Strategy Note

Not the hill we die on. DTC-first thesis is the hedge. Bass Pro on our terms, not theirs.

New Retail Development

No active recruitment until Month 6+. Exception: inbound inquiries get worked immediately.

When we expand:

  • Regional sporting goods chains in catfish country
  • Independent bait shops near Rusty's tournaments
  • Online retailers (Amazon, Walmart.com, specialty fishing sites) — strategic decision Year 2 based on DTC margin protection

Section 07

Customer Experience

CX Principle

Retention is worth more than acquisition. Especially in a 23% churn industry. Every touchpoint should earn the repeat.

Unboxing

Simple branded box. Tissue wrap. Sticker pack. Handwritten (or printed to look handwritten) thank-you card in Rusty's voice. A "tips from the pro-staff" card for first-time buyers.

Post-Purchase Email Flow

# Email Timing Purpose
1 Order confirmation + shipping Immediately Transactional. Sets expectation.
2 "How to use it" 3 days after ship Product education. Reduce regret.
3 Review request 7–10 days after delivery Social proof building. Timed to use.
4 Reorder nudge 30–45 days post-purchase LTV play. Hits when product runs low.
5 Pro-staff content share 45–60 days post-purchase Community reinforcement. Aspirational.
6 Win-back 90 days if no repeat Last attempt before churn. Incentive offer.

Reviews

  • Yotpo or Judge.me integration
  • Request email timed to when product should have been used
  • Feature best reviews in email, on-site, and in ads (with permission)

Subscription Program (Month 6–9 Launch)

  • "Catfish Box" — monthly delivery of core SKUs + seasonal rotation
  • 10–15% discount vs one-time purchase
  • Gift subscription option
  • Cancel any time
  • Target: 20–30% of Year 2 revenue from subscription

Loyalty Program (Year 2)

  • Points for purchases, reviews, referrals, social tags
  • Early access to new SKUs for loyalty members
  • Pro-staff feature consideration for top loyalty members

Measurement

Figure 04
Measurement Framework
Weekly

  • Wholesale revenue vs target
  • Website traffic (sessions, source)
  • Conversion rate
  • Average order value
  • Email list growth
  • Social engagement rate

Monthly

Quarterly

  • Net Promoter Score
  • Brand mention volume (unprompted)
  • Pro-staff program health (output vs commitment, roster changes)
  • Retail footprint growth
  • Product margin by SKU
  • Category share (directional)
The Two Metrics That Matter Most
Month 6–12
Pro-staff content output

Every piece is an asset that sells product for 12–24 months. Leading indicator of everything else.

Month 12+
Repeat purchase rate

If people buy once and don't come back, the brand isn't working regardless of top-line revenue. Target 35%+ 90-day repeat rate by Month 12, 45%+ by Month 24.

Weekly pulse keeps execution honest. Monthly shows levers. Quarterly reveals whether the brand is actually building.
